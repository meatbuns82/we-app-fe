# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small teams, families, and group households (e.g., 2-6 people) who order food together daily. Each person browses, picks dishes, and the group sees a combined order. The app is used casually throughout the day — morning meal planning, lunch decisions, evening summaries. Users access it on mobile (WeChat) in short bursts: while commuting, during breaks, or at home.

## Product Purpose

A lightweight group food-ordering tool that turns "what do we eat today?" from a decision burden into a fun daily ritual. Users browse menus, add/remove their picks, save favorites, and view the group's combined order for the day. Success means making meal decisions faster, more collaborative, and slightly more delightful.

## Brand Personality

Minimalist, refreshing, soothing. Calm clarity with moments of unexpected charm. The interface should feel like a breath of fresh air — clean lines, generous whitespace, subtle warmth. Not loud, not corporate, not generic. Quiet confidence with a gentle personality.

- **Refreshing**: Light, airy, uncluttered. Like opening a window.
- **Soothing**: Soft curves, warm neutrals, gentle transitions. Reduces decision fatigue.
- **Minimalist**: Every element earns its place. Purposeful whitespace. No decoration without function.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (too cold, too corporate)
- Food delivery apps like Meituan / Ele.me (too busy, too commercial, too much visual noise)
- Overly cute "couples" apps (too saccharine, feels artificial)
- Dark-mode-heavy UI (not right for daytime food decisions)
- Cards-with-side-stripes, gradient text, hero-metric templates — the impeccable absolute bans

## Design Principles

1. **Purposeful whitespace**: Let content breathe. Generous spacing creates calm. Don't fill gaps with decoration.
2. **One clear action per view**: Every page has one primary thing the user should do. Make it obvious.
3. **Food first, chrome second**: Photos and dish names lead. Navigation, buttons, and chrome recede.
4. **Consistency as trust**: Same spacing, same radii, same rhythm everywhere. Users should feel the system, not think about it.
5. **Delight in restraint**: Charm comes from craft — a smooth transition, perfect alignment, a well-chosen word — not from flourishes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast for all text
- Large enough touch targets (≥44pt for interactive elements)
- Support system font scaling
- No critical info conveyed through color alone
